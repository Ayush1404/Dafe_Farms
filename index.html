Climate justice for affected farming communities

Climate change has increased the frequency and coverage of drought in Maharashtra. Almost 64% of land is now under drought-prone region. This has been been linked to an increased risk of suicide among small-scale farmers.

Between January and August 2023, the central region of Maharashtra, India, witnessed a tragic toll, with over 650 farmers resorting to suicide. This concerning trend directly raises crucial human rights concerns, specifically regarding the fundamental right to life. Additionally, it highlights the fact that those who contribute the least to climate change, suffer the most.

Our carbon projects extend a lifeline to farmers battling climate hardships, intertwining environmental action with economic relief. By rewarding sustainable practices, these initiatives bolster farmers' incomes while combating carbon emissions. Engaging in carbon programs equips farming communities with resilience, knowledge, and vital skills for enduring climate challenges.
